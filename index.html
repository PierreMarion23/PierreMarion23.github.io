Pierre Marion

I am a PhD student in Machine Learning at Sorbonne University, and an engineer for the French State (Corps des Mines). I am working under the supervision of Gérard Biau and Jean-Philippe Vert.

My current research interests are about the links between deep learning and classical analysis of dynamical systems. For more information on my research activity, you can visit the Research page.

Some cool projects I worked on

Building custom Jupyter widgets

Jupyter widgets enable interactive data visualization in the Jupyter notebooks.

Computing highly-uniform point sets

LatNet Builder is a state-of-the-art C++ software tool for constructing highly-uniform point sets for quasi-Monte Carlo methods.

Question Answering grounded in Knowledge Graphs

Knowledge Graphs are a common format to store real-world information at scale, and querying them to answer factual questions is useful for voice assistants.
